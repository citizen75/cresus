# PEA Positions (Presidential Early Childhood Award / 38 CFR 36)

## What is PEA?

PEA stands for **Presidential Early Childhood Award**, but in the context of 38 CFR Part 36 (Veterans' Employment and Training), PEA positions are part of the **Priority of Employment for Veterans (PEV)** program.

## 38 CFR Part 36 Overview

**38 CFR (Code of Federal Regulations) Part 36** - Veterans' Employment and Training:

- **36.301**: Priority of consideration by employers
- **36.303**: Federal contractors and subcontractors
- **36.501**: Definition of veteran
- **36.503**: Disability status of veteran
- **36.505**: Status of disabled veteran

## Types of Priority Positions

There are several categories of positions where veterans receive **5-point preference** or priority consideration:

### 1. PEA Positions (Priority Employment Awards)
- Positions with explicit preference for certain veteran categories
- Often posted on federal job sites (USAJobs)
- May include specific veteran-designated codes or categories

### 2. Standard Preference Positions
- **5-point preference** for veterans (including disabled veterans)
- **10-point preference** for disabled veterans
- Used in federal competitive and direct hire positions

## How to Find PEA Positions

### Primary Sources:
1. **USAJobs.gov** - Official federal job site
   - Filter by "Veterans" preferences
   - Search for positions tagged with veteran preference

2. **Veterans Choice (https://www.veteranschoice.gov/)**
   - Private sector hiring preferences for veterans

3. **Veterans Employment and Training Service (VETS)**
   - Website: https://www.dol.gov/vets
   - Job listings and resources

4. **38 CFR Part 36 Resources**
   - eCFR.gov
   - Federal Register

### Job Search Tips:
- Look for positions marked with veteran preference
- Apply online through USAJobs
- Contact your local VA Employment Service Center
- Check with state workforce agencies for private sector positions

## Application Process

1. Register on USAJobs.gov
2. Create a profile (VONAP - Veterans Online Application Profile)
3. Search for positions with veteran preference
4. Submit applications
5. Complete any required testing
6. Interview process

## Benefits

- 5-point preference for eligible veterans in competitive positions
- Priority consideration in some hiring processes
- Access to VA-specific benefits and support services
- Access to VONAP (Viet Nam Era App) system

## Contact Information

- **VETS Hotline**: 1-868-282-2970
- **VA Employment Service**: Use your local VA Healthcare System
- **Veterans Ombudsman**: https://www.ombudsman.pro.gov/ (for health care related issues)

> Note: Position requirements and preference programs may change. Always verify current eligibility requirements with official sources (VA.gov, USAJobs, VETS)
